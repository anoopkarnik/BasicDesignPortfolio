There are two main categories of raw materials or icon making. The first category consists of metals like copper, brass, lead, tin, zinc, silver and gold, which are used for casting, the second category consists of materials like bees-wax (thennmezhuku), dammar (kunkulium) and groundnut oil (kadala ennai) which are used for making wax models. The subsidiary raw materials are charcoal, firewood cow dung cakes, tamarind, metal wires of different gauges and clay of different varieties.

The quantities of metal were and other raw materials required for each icon depending on the size of the icon. But there exists an exact proportion between the weight of the metals to be used for casting a particular icon and the weight of the wax model used for the same icon. The metal used for casting are purchased either in the form of solid pieces or in the form of scraps. generally, the purchases are made either at Kumbakonam or at Tanjore. The prices of solid metal and scrap metal don't differ much, but a good craftsman generally does not like to use scrap metal as icons cast of scraps would not give good shine when polished. Bees-wax is purchased at Kumbakonam market while dammer and groundnut oil are purchased either at Kumbakonam or locally.
