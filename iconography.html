ARDHANAREESWARA

Fashioned in Utthama dasa tala. 14. The lord who is half woman is an androgynous form of Lord Shiva and his consort Parvathi. It represents the aesthetic symbiosis of masculine and feminine energies in the universe. The masculine side representing Shiva, has half third eye, Thrishool (trident) in one arm and another palm depicting varada mudra (blessing gesture) whereas Parvathi’s part is sculpted in a more composite form. This composition symbolises the inseparable balance that the unity of male and female beholds, in the universe.

BUDDHA

The divine image of Gautama Buddha should be fashioned in Utthama dash tala. The image of Buddha should have a broad face, elongated eyes, a prominent nose, long and shapely ears, with a gentle smile on the lips. The arms should be rounded and long. The chest should be fash- ioned with great beauty. The various parts of the body should be well formed and rounded and the stomach should be clearly indicated. The skin should be white in colour and a mark called urna should be placed on the forehead between the eyebrows which is a tuft of hair. The other features of Buddha image are curly hair, coiled bun on top of the head, yellow silken cloth below the waist, an upper garment fastened on the left shoulder and draped to the right waist. The end go the garment should be pleated and gracefully fixed on the shoulder.

NATARAJA

1. Ring of Cosmic Fire It represents the cosmic fire to destroy the universe as a part of the mighty cycle of destruction and creation, each flame holding three points.
2. Third eye The third eye, also known as ‘jnana caksu’- meaning eye of wisdom, is a representation of Lord Shiva’s cosmic and universal knowledge and power.
3. Right Hand The open right palm showcases the ‘abhaya’ mudra depicting the futility of fear in the minds of his

worshippers.
4. Clothing He is adorned with a veshti around his waist and yajnopavita (the sacred thread of the Brahmin priestly class).
5. Drum The damaru or the drum represents the cycle of creation and destruction. The rhythmic drum beats of Shiva, calls for the creation of the universe.
6. Left Foot Shiva’s left foot raised sideways, portrays a posture in His Dance of Bliss. It signifies upliftment and liberation.
7.Lotus Base Lotus, a symbol of purity in the Hindu, Jain and Buddhist traditions is believed to have a divine and pristine beauty, emerging from the mud beneath us.
8.Crescent moon The crescent moon in his matted hair keeps Kama, the god of nightly love, alive. Through the waxing and the waning of the moon, Shiva creates different seasons and rejuvenates life.
9.Ganga The River Ganges is personified in the form of a goddess resting on Shiva’s matted hair. Mythology speaks that in a drought situation Ganga agreed to come down to Earth and nourish its beings.
10.Demon of Ignorance
11.Flame of Destruction Beholding the flame of cosmic fire, Shiva triumphs it to destroy the universe.
12.Multiple Arms Highlighting His divine power, four arms of Nataraja depict strength and unity of skills, through different positions or by holding symbolic objects.
13.Left Hand The upper left hand makes a half- moon gesture called ‘ardhachandra mudra’. The flame held in his palm represents ‘Pralayagni’, the fire that destroys the world.
14.Right Foot The right foot rests on the back of the dwarfish demon – ‘Apasmarupasura’, whom Shiva conquers.
15.Apasmarupasura is a personification of illusion and ignorance. The demon symbolises the ignorance which makes one loose their balance and consciousness. The demon also symbolises ego which is conquered and crushed at the feet of Nataraja.
16.Matted locks (Jata) The matted locks is a symbol of Shiva being a yogi in the Himalayas. The latter half of it flies in vigour as He performs the Thanadava (dance), crash into the heavenly bodies and destroying them.
17.Head Wheel (rear) The halo adorning the God is created by an artisan to cover the knot of the headband.
18.Earrings His earring on the right side is a depiction of a mythical water creature, Makara. His left earring is similar to that worn by a woman, demonstrating Shiva as a half- male, half- female depiction with Parvathi.
